CJ 351: Stategic Event Planning

In this course, students are given the opportunity to fully plan GEEKcon, a campus event that attracts over 1500 attendiees from all over. In this class, Ellen was the Director of Publicity and Promotion. While in this role, Ellen collaberated and managed a team of six students where they worked on graphic design, digital and print advertisements, interviews with local news and radio stations, donations and sponcerships with companies, and managing social media pages. Along with being a director, Ellen also acted as the EmCee of the event. Ellen created a script and slideshow to increase engamement from attendies, preformed trivia, acted as the main source of information, and announced important information to everyone attending. While being in this class, Ellen learned skills that included leadership, collaberation, time management, community engagement, press releases, and public speaking.

CJ 374: ISC Campaign, Design, and Evaluation

In this course, students are placed into groups and are given a client to create a new campaign to increase community engagement. For Ellen’s class, the client was the University Bookstore. The Bookstore in this situation was struggling with faculty and student engagement, resulting in purchasing textbooks, other reading material, and merchendise from other retailers. The overall goal of this course is to increase understanding of the public communication campaign process and to provide a substantive foundation of relevant research related to the development, implementation, and evaluation of strategig communication (advertising and public relations) campaigns. While being in this class, Ellen has gained experience in group collaberation, data analysis, problem solving, and client engagement.
